How to Replace Front Brake Pads — 2014 Honda Accord

  1. Park on level ground, set the parking brake, and loosen the front lug nuts.
  2. Raise the vehicle with a floor jack and support with jack stands. Remove the front wheels.
  3. Remove the lower caliper guide pin bolt (14mm). Pivot the caliper up and support it — do not hang by the hose.
  4. Slide out the old pads and note pad wear indicator orientation.
  5. Compress the caliper piston with a C-clamp or piston tool. Keep the reservoir cap loose.
  6. Install new pads with shims/grease as specified. Torque guide pin bolt to 25 ft-lbs (34 N·m).
  7. Reinstall the wheel. Torque lug nuts to 80 ft-lbs (108 N·m) in a star pattern.
  8. Pump the brake pedal until firm before driving. Road-test at low speed.

Tools: 14mm socket, C-clamp, torque wrench, jack stands.
